Zhen Wang 王震

Postdoctoral Researcher, UC San Diego

I develop the computational foundations of intelligent systems, focusing on human-AI knowledge co-evolution. My research seeks to answer fundamental questions: How do models represent knowledge? How do they reason and plan to extrapolate? How do they collaborate reliably with humans?

My philosophy centers on bringing new perspectives to shift existing paradigms—exemplified by RAP (reasoning as planning), ToolkenGPT (tools as tokens), and PromptAgent (prompting as search).

I earned my PhD from The Ohio State University, developing foundational frameworks for knowledge-centric AI systems. My work has been recognized with the Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation Fellowship, OpenAI Agentic AI Research Award, Rising Star in Data Science (UChicago), Best Paper Award at SoCal NLP, and Amazon Alexa Prize. I collaborate with MIT-IBM Lab, Microsoft Research, NEC Labs, and academic institutions including UCSD, CMU, and MBZUAI.

This space extends traditional data-driven discovery, calling for AI systems that actively explore hypotheses, simulate possibilities, and generate new, verifiable knowledge across disciplines.
